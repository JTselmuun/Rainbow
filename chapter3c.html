That night, I couldn't stop thinking about it, not because something bad happened, but because something almost happened.

Later, when we talked again, you told me that you weren't ready for one-on-one meetings. I understood, or at least I tried to.

But at the same time, I had already prepared myself for it, so hearing that felt a little strange. Not painful, just unexpected.

I asked you a few simple questions after that, trying to understand you a little more, but I stopped myself from asking too much because I didn't want to be annoying or make things uncomfortable.

There were still many things I wanted to ask, but I kept most of them to myself.

Even then, I didn't feel like stopping, because something had begun to spark.

It wasn't clear, and it wasn't certain, but it was enough to keep me moving forward.
