At that time, it didn't feel important. But looking back, that small decision changed everything.That was when I met you.

Meeting you didn't feel normal. It wasn't something I expected, and it didn't feel like just another moment. It felt different in a way I couldn't explain.

It felt like a miracle.

I saw you walking toward our pavilion, and I got into position. You looked Mongolian, and I wanted to be sure, so I checked your name tag before saying "Hello" in Mongolian. When you looked at me, something about that moment stayed.

You were beautiful, but not in a way that tried to stand out. It was something softer, something that stayed even after you looked away. You reminded me of a rainbow. Maybe it was because a rainbow only appears when rain meets sunshine, and somehow, you felt like both at the same time.

Before I even realized it, my eyes were following you. I watched you until you left, noticing small things without thinking too much about it-the way you reacted, the way you spoke-and somehow, I wanted to know more.

At some point, I realized I was smiling. Just from looking at you.
