Chapter 3: Yellow - Light

After that, we didn't have any real conversations, and most of the time it was just me sending a message and you seeing it. There wasn't much between us on the surface, but somehow, it didn't feel empty. Because something stayed with me.

It was your smile.

I didn't see it again, but it stayed in my mind every day. The way you smiled that day felt simple and natural, but for some reason, I kept going back to it. Even when nothing new happened between us.

Sometimes I thought about messaging you more, asking questions, trying to continue something, but I stopped myself. I didn't want to be annoying or make things uncomfortable, so I held back, even when I didn't want to. Even then, I still found myself checking my phone more than before, even though I already knew how it would go.

But even in that quiet space between us, there were still small moments that stayed.

I remember making a cake for you, even though I wasn't sure how it would turn out. I hadn't done something like that in a long time, and I knew it wouldn't be perfect. Still, I wanted to try.
